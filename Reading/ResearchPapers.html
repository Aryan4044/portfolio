Entropy-Enhanced Random Number Generation

This paper proposes a lightweight hybrid RNG architecture that fuses a software PRNG with analog noise sampled via a low-cost Arduino microcontroller. Our design uses bitwise XOR and SHA-256 hashing to produce output with enhanced randomness approaching the ideal of 8 bits of Shannon entropy per byte – making it highly suitable for real-time embedded and IoT security. Experimental evaluation confirms substantial improvements in randomness quality and resilience to seed compromise compared to standalone solutions.

Re-living your childhood: Retropie

This document explores the nostalgic joy of 90s gaming, contrasting it with modern gaming's focus on intense realism. It presents RetroPie as a solution to revisit classic games on a single device, offering a vast library of titles. The guide also details how to integrate open-source remakes as "Ports" for enhanced retro experiences. Quick steps for installing games on RetroPie are provided, covering game selection, checking RetroPie Ports, manual installation processes, and enabling the "Ports" section in Emulation Station to play the games

Rise of Artificial Intelligence: A Deep Dive into Data Selection and Training

This article discusses key insights into Artificial Intelligence (AI) , focusing on the crucial aspects of dataset selection and training. It defines AI as a broad field aiming for human-like intelligence in machines, with Machine Learning (ML) as its subset where machines learn from data without explicit programming. The three main methods of ML—supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning—are covered. The document emphasizes that data is crucial for AI/ML , detailing factors for selecting the right dataset (relevance, size, quality, diversity) and categorizing data into training, validation, and test sets for effective model development.
